🚕 Taxi Demand Forecasting: Time Series Regression & Neural Networks📌 Project Overview & Business ValueThe primary objective of this project is to accurately forecast hourly taxi demand using historical ride data.  Business Problem: A mismatch between rider demand and driver supply leads to long rider wait times, driver idle time, and lost revenue.  Business Goal: Minimize operational inefficiencies by predicting demand with an internal target of RMSE < 40 orders per hour.  Methodology: The project was executed using Agile/Scrum framework across two 1-week sprints using Jira, where I served as the Scrum Master for a team of 7.  📊 Data Dynamics & Feature EngineeringThe raw dataset contained high-frequency records logged every 10 minutes. To observe clear trends and build a robust model, the following pipeline was implemented:  Resampling & EDA: Data was aggregated into 1-hour intervals. Time series seasonal decomposition (period = 24) was applied to isolate deep daily seasonality and complex categorical features.  Feature Engineering: To capture temporal dependencies, several key features were extracted:Calendar features: hour, dayofweek, is_weekend.  Lagged features: Generated historical lags up to 24 hours (max_lag=24).  Window features: Calculated a 24-hour rolling mean (rolling_mean_size=24) to smooth out short-term fluctuations.  🚀 Machine Learning & ResultsThe baseline model (predicting demand based on the exact previous hour) yielded a critical error of RMSE = 58. Several models were tested to beat this baseline and achieve the internal < 40 target:  Linear Regression: 40.5 (Captured basic trends but underfitted on non-linear spikes).  CatBoost: 39.5 (Successfully captured daily seasonality and categorical features).  Random Forest: 37.6 (Excellent at capturing daily seasonality, but limited by inability to extrapolate beyond historical maximums).  🏆 Neural Network: 7.9 (Winner. Excelled at finding hidden temporal dependencies across multiple lag features simultaneously, generalizing complex sequential patterns).  Conclusion: The Neural Network successfully reduced the forecasting error from 58 to 7.9, providing a highly accurate predictive tool to optimize pricing and improve driver utilization.
